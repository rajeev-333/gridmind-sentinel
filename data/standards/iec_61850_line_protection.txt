IEC 61850 — Transmission Line Protection Schemes
===================================================

Section 1: Scope

This document describes the application of IEC 61850-based protection schemes
for high-voltage transmission lines, including distance protection, differential
protection, and pilot-aided schemes. It covers the logical node models, GOOSE
communication requirements, and protection coordination for multi-terminal
line configurations.

Section 2: Distance Protection (ANSI 21)

2.1 Operating Principle
Distance relays measure the apparent impedance (V/I) at the relay location.
A fault on the protected line causes a drop in measured impedance proportional
to the distance from the relay to the fault point.

2.2 Zone Settings
  Zone 1: Set to reach 80-85% of the protected line impedance.
          Operating time: instantaneous (< 30 ms after fault inception).
          Purpose: primary protection for close-in and mid-line faults.

  Zone 2: Set to reach 120-150% of the protected line impedance.
          Operating time: 0.3-0.5 seconds time delay.
          Purpose: backup for remote end of line and part of adjacent line.

  Zone 3: Set to reach 100% of adjacent line impedance.
          Operating time: 0.8-1.2 seconds time delay.
          Purpose: remote backup (use with caution — may operate on load).

2.3 Mho Characteristic
The standard mho relay characteristic is a circle passing through the origin
of the R-X diagram, with the diameter equal to the relay reach setting. The
relay operates when the measured impedance falls within this circle.

Section 3: Line Differential Protection (ANSI 87L)

3.1 Operating Principle
Line differential protection compares the current entering and leaving the
protected line (Kirchhoff's Current Law). If the differential current exceeds
a threshold, an internal fault is declared.

  I_diff = |I_local + I_remote|
  I_restraint = |I_local| + |I_remote|
  Operate condition: I_diff > k × I_restraint + I_min

Where k is the percentage restraint slope (typically 20-40%) and I_min is
the minimum pickup current (typically 0.2-0.5 pu).

3.2 Communication Requirements
Per IEC 61850-9-2 (Sampled Values), the protection relay at each terminal
must exchange current samples at 80 samples per cycle (4000 samples/second
for 50 Hz systems). Communication latency must be < 5 ms for correct
differential comparison. Channel delay asymmetry must be < 1 ms.

3.3 Advantages Over Distance Protection
  - No zone coordination required (absolute selectivity)
  - Correct operation for all fault types including high-impedance faults
  - Not affected by power swings or load flow
  - No risk of over-reach or under-reach due to mutual coupling

Section 4: Pilot-Aided Protection Schemes

4.1 Permissive Overreach Transfer Trip (POTT)
  Step 1: Local relay detects fault in Zone 2 (overreaching direction).
  Step 2: Local relay sends permissive trip signal to remote terminal.
  Step 3: Remote relay detects fault in its Zone 2 — confirms direction.
  Step 4: Upon receiving permissive signal AND local detection — trips instantly.
  
  Advantages: Fast clearance for all internal faults. Secure against
  external faults (requires confirmation from both ends).

4.2 Directional Comparison Blocking (DCB)
  Step 1: Local relay detects fault in overreaching zone.
  Step 2: If remote relay sees fault in reverse direction — sends BLOCK signal.
  Step 3: If no blocking signal received within 15-30 ms — local relay trips.
  
  Advantages: Functions on loss of communication channel (trips if no block
  received). Suitable for single-channel pilot schemes.

Section 5: IEC 61850 Logical Nodes for Line Protection

  PDIS — Distance protection function
  PDIF — Differential protection function
  PTRC — Protection trip conditioning
  PSCH — Protection scheme logic (pilot-aided)
  RBRF — Breaker failure protection
  RADR — Autorecloser function

Each logical node is mapped to the IEC 61850 data model with standardized
data objects and attributes, ensuring interoperability between relays from
different manufacturers.

Section 6: Autoreclosing (ANSI 79)

For transient faults (80-90% of line faults are transient due to lightning
or tree contact), auto-reclosing is applied:
  - Single-shot reclose: dead time = 0.5-1.5 seconds
  - Three-phase reclose: dead time = 1.0-3.0 seconds
  - Synchro-check before reclose: angle difference < 20°, voltage > 0.85 pu
  - If fault persists after reclose: lockout — manual investigation

Section 7: Fault Location

Impedance-based fault location: distance = (Z_measured / Z_line) × line_length
Two-ended fault location (more accurate):
  d = (Z_measured_local - Z_measured_remote + Z_total) / (2 × Z_total) × length

Accuracy target: ±1% of line length for solid faults, ±3% for high-impedance.
