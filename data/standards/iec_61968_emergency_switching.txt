IEC 61968 — Emergency Switching Procedures and Safety Protocols
================================================================

Section 1: Scope

This document defines the emergency switching procedures for medium-voltage
and high-voltage power distribution systems per IEC 61968 (Application
Integration at Electric Utilities), IEC 62271 (High-Voltage Switchgear and
Controlgear), and applicable safety regulations. Emergency switching is
performed to isolate faults, restore service, or protect personnel, and
carries inherent risk of equipment damage or personnel injury if not
executed correctly.

Section 2: Types of Emergency Switching Operations

2.1 Fault Isolation Switching
Performed to isolate a faulted section of the network to:
  - Prevent damage propagation to adjacent equipment
  - Protect personnel from energized fault conditions
  - Enable safe access for repair crews

2.2 Emergency Load Transfer
Performed when a transformer or feeder must be de-energized urgently:
  - Transfer load to alternate supply before de-energization
  - Sequence: close tie → verify parallel operation → open original supply

2.3 Emergency De-Energization
Performed when immediate disconnection is required:
  - Equipment fire or explosion
  - Live conductor down on public area
  - Oil spill from transformer (fire/environmental hazard)
  - Personnel electric shock incident

Section 3: Safety Prerequisites

BEFORE any switching operation, the following MUST be verified:

3.1 Authorization
  - All switching operations require authorization from the control center.
  - CRITICAL operations (de-energization, breaker operation, transformer
    isolation) require supervisor approval.
  - Written switching order must be issued and read back by the field operator.

3.2 Equipment Verification
  - Verify correct identification of the equipment to be switched.
  - Confirm single-line diagram matches field conditions.
  - Check protection relay settings and status indicators.
  - Verify that automatic reclosing is DISABLED on all relevant breakers
    before manual switching begins.

3.3 Personnel Safety
  - All personnel must wear appropriate PPE (Class E gloves, face shield,
    flame-resistant clothing) for the voltage class being switched.
  - Minimum approach distances per IEC 61472:
    11 kV: 0.7 m     33 kV: 1.0 m     66 kV: 1.4 m
    132 kV: 2.0 m    220 kV: 3.0 m    400 kV: 4.5 m
  - A safety observer must be present during all HV switching operations.

Section 4: Emergency Switching Procedure

Step 1: RECEIVE — Emergency switching request received from operator or
        automatic protection system.

Step 2: ASSESS — Evaluate the urgency and scope:
  - Immediate danger to life? → proceed to emergency de-energization
  - Equipment at risk? → determine isolation boundary
  - Can load be transferred first? → minimize customer outage

Step 3: AUTHORIZE — Obtain switching order from control center:
  - Identify all switches/breakers in the switching sequence
  - Specify the sequence of operations (open/close order)
  - Identify isolation points and grounding locations

Step 4: COMMUNICATE — Notify all affected parties:
  - Field crews in the vicinity
  - Adjacent control centers (for inter-tie operations)
  - Customer service (for planned outage notification if time permits)

Step 5: EXECUTE — Perform switching operations:
  - Execute each step of the switching order in sequence
  - Provide positive confirmation after each operation
  - Verify de-energization with voltage detector at each isolation point
  - Apply safety grounds after confirmed de-energization

Step 6: VERIFY — Post-switching verification:
  - Confirm all intended circuits are de-energized/energized correctly
  - Check voltage levels and loading on transferred circuits
  - Verify no overloads on alternate supply paths

Step 7: DOCUMENT — Record all details:
  - Switching order number and authorization
  - Exact time of each switching operation
  - Equipment operated (device ID, location)
  - Personnel involved
  - Any abnormal conditions observed

Section 5: Prohibited Operations

The following operations are PROHIBITED without explicit engineering approval:
  - Closing a breaker onto a known fault condition
  - Operating a switch under load beyond its rated breaking capacity
  - Energizing a grounded circuit
  - Paralleling two sources without synchronism check
  - Operating equipment with known defects (broken insulators, leaking oil)

All prohibited operation attempts must be logged as safety incidents.

Section 6: Post-Emergency Restoration

After the emergency condition is resolved:
  Step 1: Remove safety grounds in reverse order of application.
  Step 2: Verify insulation integrity before re-energization (megger test
          if equipment was opened or exposed).
  Step 3: Re-energize in stages — energize each section under no-load first,
          then restore load incrementally.
  Step 4: Monitor for 15 minutes after restoration — verify voltage and
          current are within normal ranges.
  Step 5: Re-enable automatic reclosing on relevant breakers.
  Step 6: Submit post-incident report within 24 hours.
