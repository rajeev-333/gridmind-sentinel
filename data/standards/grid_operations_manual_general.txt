Grid Operations Manual — General Operating Procedures
======================================================

Section 1: Scope

This manual provides the general operating procedures for power grid control
center operations, applicable to all voltage levels from distribution (11 kV)
to transmission (400 kV). It covers normal operations, abnormal conditions,
system monitoring, and coordination protocols between control areas.

Section 2: Normal Operating Conditions

2.1 System Parameters — Normal Ranges
  Voltage:   0.95 - 1.05 per unit (± 5% of nominal)
  Current:   0.00 - 1.00 per unit (below thermal rating)
  Frequency: 49.80 - 50.20 Hz (for 50 Hz systems)
  Power Factor: 0.85 - 1.00 lagging (normal load)
  Active Power: Within scheduled dispatch ± 5%
  Reactive Power: Within voltage schedule requirements

2.2 Monitoring Intervals
  SCADA scan rate: 2-4 seconds for critical measurements
  Operator review cycle: Every 30 minutes (normal conditions)
  Load forecast update: Every hour
  Equipment inspection: Daily visual, monthly detailed

Section 3: Abnormal Condition Response

3.1 Severity Classification

  LOW: Parameter slightly outside normal range. No immediate risk.
       Example: Voltage at 0.93 pu (below 0.95 but above 0.90).
       Action: Log and monitor. Auto-regulation expected to correct.

  MEDIUM: Parameter significantly outside normal range. Equipment stress.
          Example: Feeder loading at 110% for 30+ minutes.
          Action: Alert operator. Initiate corrective actions within 15 min.

  HIGH: Parameter at alarm level. Equipment or system at risk.
        Example: Transformer hot spot > 130°C, or voltage < 0.85 pu.
        Action: Immediate operator response. Supervisor notification.
               Initiate load transfer or generation redispatch.

  CRITICAL: Parameter at emergency level. Imminent failure or safety risk.
            Example: Line fault with sustained arc, or frequency < 49.0 Hz.
            Action: Automatic protection operation. Human-in-the-loop for
                    any switching decisions. Senior engineer approval required.

3.2 Escalation Protocol
  Step 1: Control room operator detects abnormal condition.
  Step 2: Classify severity per Section 3.1.
  Step 3: If MEDIUM or higher — notify shift supervisor.
  Step 4: If HIGH — notify area manager and engineering support.
  Step 5: If CRITICAL — notify all management levels and regulatory body.

Section 4: Load Management

4.1 Load Shedding Priority
When emergency load shedding is required, follow this priority sequence
(shed lowest priority first):
  Priority 1 (shed first): Industrial non-essential loads
  Priority 2: Commercial air conditioning and non-critical loads
  Priority 3: Residential non-essential loads
  Priority 4: Industrial essential loads (continuous process)
  Priority 5 (shed last): Hospitals, emergency services, water treatment

4.2 Restoration Priority
When restoring load after an emergency, reverse the shedding order:
  Restore Priority 5 first (critical loads), then 4, 3, 2, 1.
  Restore in 5% increments with 2-minute intervals between stages to
  avoid cold load pickup surges.

Section 5: Communication Protocols

5.1 Operational Communication
  - All switching orders: read back and confirm protocol.
  - Use standard phonetic alphabet for equipment identifiers.
  - Three-way communication: instruct → repeat back → confirm.
  - All communications logged with timestamp.

5.2 Emergency Communication
  - Use dedicated emergency radio channel.
  - State "EMERGENCY" at the start of each emergency transmission.
  - Identify: Who is calling, what happened, where, and what is needed.
  - Maintain communication channel open until emergency resolved.

Section 6: Incident Reporting

6.1 Immediate Report
Within 1 hour of any significant event:
  - Event type and location
  - Time of occurrence and duration
  - Equipment affected
  - Customers affected (number and MW)
  - Immediate actions taken

6.2 Detailed Report
Within 24 hours:
  - Root cause analysis (preliminary)
  - Chronological sequence of events
  - Protection relay and breaker operation records
  - Corrective actions planned
  - Recommendations for prevention

6.3 Investigation Report
Within 30 days for HIGH and CRITICAL events:
  - Complete root cause analysis
  - Equipment inspection and test results
  - Protection system performance assessment
  - Regulatory compliance review
  - Long-term corrective actions and timeline

Section 7: Shift Operations

7.1 Shift Handover
  - Outgoing operator briefs incoming operator on:
    - Current system status (all abnormal conditions)
    - Ongoing events or maintenance activities
    - Pending switching operations
    - Equipment out of service
    - Weather conditions affecting grid operations
  - Both operators sign the shift log.
  - Minimum 15-minute overlap for handover.
