IEC 61968 — Transformer Overload Detection and Management
===========================================================

Section 1: Scope

This document specifies the procedures for monitoring, detecting, and managing
transformer overload conditions in power distribution systems per IEC 61968
(Application Integration at Electric Utilities) and IEC 60076 (Power
Transformers). Transformer overloads degrade insulation life and may lead
to catastrophic failure if not managed properly.

Section 2: Transformer Thermal Characteristics

2.1 Temperature Limits (Oil-Immersed Transformers, per IEC 60076-7)
  - Top Oil Temperature (TOT) normal limit: 95°C ambient + 65°C rise = 105°C max
  - Hot Spot Temperature (HST) normal limit: 120°C
  - Emergency overload HST limit: 140°C (for up to 30 minutes)
  - Critical HST limit: 160°C — immediate de-energization required

2.2 Loading Limits
  Normal Continuous Rating: 100% MVA (1.00 pu load)
  Planned Overload: Up to 120% for 4 hours (1.20 pu) — per IEC 60076-7
  Emergency Overload: Up to 150% for 30 minutes (1.50 pu) — accelerated aging
  Short-Time Emergency: Up to 180% for 5 minutes (1.80 pu) — insulation risk

Section 3: Overload Detection

3.1 Current-Based Detection
The transformer load current is monitored continuously via CTs on the LV side.
An overload condition is declared when:
  Level 1 (Caution): Load current exceeds 100% of rated for > 1 hour
  Level 2 (Warning): Load current exceeds 120% of rated for > 15 minutes
  Level 3 (Alarm): Load current exceeds 140% of rated for any duration
  Level 4 (Trip): Load current exceeds 180% of rated — immediate action

3.2 Thermal Model-Based Detection
The top oil temperature and hot spot temperature are calculated using the
IEC 60076-7 thermal model, which considers:
  - Ambient temperature
  - Load current (square law heating)
  - Thermal time constants (oil: 60-180 min, winding: 5-10 min)
  - Cooling mode (ONAN, ONAF, OFAF)

3.3 Dissolved Gas Analysis (DGA)
For chronic overload conditions, periodic DGA sampling of transformer oil
detects incipient faults. Key gas indicators:
  - Hydrogen (H2): partial discharge or overheating
  - Ethylene (C2H4): hot metal overheating (> 300°C)
  - Acetylene (C2H2): arcing — requires immediate investigation

Section 4: Overload Management Procedures

4.1 Level 1 Response (Caution — 100-120%)
  Step 1: Log the overload event with timestamp and loading level.
  Step 2: Check cooling system status — ensure all fans/pumps are running.
  Step 3: Switch cooling mode from ONAN to ONAF if available.
  Step 4: Monitor top oil temperature trend — no immediate action if
          temperature is within normal limits.

4.2 Level 2 Response (Warning — 120-140%)
  Step 1: Alert operator via SCADA alarm.
  Step 2: Verify ambient temperature — hotter ambient reduces overload capacity.
  Step 3: Initiate load transfer to reduce loading on the overloaded unit:
          - Identify parallel transformer or alternate feeder path
          - Transfer 20-30% of load to bring the unit below 100%
  Step 4: If load transfer is not possible, prepare for load shedding on
          non-critical feeders.

4.3 Level 3 Response (Alarm — 140-180%)
  Step 1: Immediate operator notification — supervisor approval required.
  Step 2: Forced load shedding — disconnect lowest priority feeders to
          reduce load below 120%.
  Step 3: Monitor hot spot temperature — if HST exceeds 140°C, prepare
          for de-energization.
  Step 4: Send field crew to inspect transformer for signs of distress
          (oil leaks, discoloration, abnormal noise).

4.4 Level 4 Response (Trip — >180%)
  Step 1: Automatic protection relay operation — transformer trip.
  Step 2: Verify all breakers have opened successfully.
  Step 3: Block automatic reclosing — do not re-energize until inspected.
  Step 4: Initiate emergency load transfer to restore supply via
          alternate transformers.
  Step 5: Conduct post-trip inspection: oil temperature, DGA sampling,
          winding resistance measurement.

Section 5: Insulation Life Assessment

Per IEC 60076-7, the relative aging rate of transformer insulation doubles
for every 6°C rise in hot spot temperature above 98°C:
  - At 98°C HST: aging rate = 1.0 (normal life: 20-30 years)
  - At 104°C HST: aging rate = 2.0 (twice normal aging)
  - At 110°C HST: aging rate = 4.0
  - At 140°C HST: aging rate = hundreds — emergency only

The accumulated loss of life must be tracked and reported quarterly.
