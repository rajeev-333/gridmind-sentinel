IEEE P2030 — Smart Grid Monitoring and Distributed Energy Resource Integration
================================================================================

Section 1: Scope

This document provides guidelines for monitoring and control of smart grid
systems with integrated Distributed Energy Resources (DER) per IEEE P2030
(Guide for Smart Grid Interoperability) and IEEE 1547 (Standard for
Interconnection and Interoperability of DER with Power Systems). It addresses
the monitoring infrastructure, data exchange protocols, and operational
procedures for grids with high DER penetration.

Section 2: Monitoring Architecture

2.1 Monitoring Layers
  Layer 1 — Field Devices: Smart meters (AMI), distribution line sensors,
            DER inverter telemetry, weather stations, PMUs.
  Layer 2 — Communication Network: Fiber optic, cellular (4G/5G), RF mesh,
            power line communication (PLC) per IEEE 1901.
  Layer 3 — Data Aggregation: SCADA master station, Distribution Management
            System (DMS), Advanced DMS (ADMS).
  Layer 4 — Analytics: State estimation, voltage optimization, fault
            prediction, DER dispatch optimization.

2.2 Key Monitored Parameters
  Bus-level: Voltage (pu), current (pu), frequency (Hz), active power (MW),
             reactive power (MVAr), power factor.
  Feeder-level: Loading percentage, loss percentage, switch status.
  DER-level: Inverter output (kW, kVAr), state of charge (SOC for batteries),
             generation forecast (solar/wind).

Section 3: DER Impact on Grid Monitoring

3.1 Voltage Rise
High DER penetration causes reverse power flow on distribution feeders,
leading to voltage rise at the point of common coupling (PCC). Monitoring
must detect:
  - Steady-state voltage exceeding 1.05 pu at any node
  - Voltage fluctuations due to cloud transients (solar PV) — rate of
    change > 0.03 pu/second
  - Voltage unbalance exceeding 2% (negative sequence / positive sequence)

3.2 Protection Coordination Challenges
DER fault current contribution alters the original protection coordination:
  - Increased fault current may cause upstream relay to trip before
    downstream fuse (fuse saving scheme failure)
  - Reduced fault current from weak grid (low short-circuit ratio) may cause
    relay under-reach
  - Bi-directional current flow requires directional overcurrent protection

3.3 Islanding Detection
Unintentional islanding must be detected within 2 seconds per IEEE 1547.
Active detection methods:
  - Sandia Frequency Shift (SFS)
  - Slip-Mode Frequency Shift (SMS)
  - Active frequency drift (AFD)
Passive detection methods:
  - Over/under voltage (OVP/UVP)
  - Over/under frequency (OFP/UFP)
  - Rate of change of frequency (ROCOF)
  - Vector surge

Section 4: Real-Time Monitoring Procedures

4.1 Normal Operations Monitoring
  Step 1: All bus voltages within 0.95-1.05 pu — GREEN status.
  Step 2: All feeder loadings below 80% of thermal rating — GREEN status.
  Step 3: DER output within forecast ± 20% — NORMAL indication.
  Step 4: System frequency within 49.95-50.05 Hz — no action.

4.2 Abnormal Condition Monitoring
  Step 5: If any bus voltage exceeds 1.05 pu — issue VOLTAGE HIGH alarm.
  Step 6: If any feeder loading exceeds 80% — issue LOADING CAUTION.
  Step 7: If DER output drops > 30% of forecast within 5 minutes — issue
          SOLAR RAMP alert — prepare spinning reserve activation.
  Step 8: If frequency deviates beyond 49.80-50.20 Hz — issue FREQUENCY
          DEVIATION alarm — escalate to balancing authority.

Section 5: Data Exchange Standards

All monitoring data shall be exchanged using:
  - IEC 61850 (substation level): GOOSE for protection, MMS for SCADA
  - IEC 61968/61970 CIM (utility level): XML/JSON data model
  - IEEE 2030.5 (DER level): Smart Energy Profile for DER management
  - OpenADR (demand response): Automated demand response signaling

Data update rates:
  - Protection signals: ≤ 4 ms (IEC 61850 GOOSE)
  - SCADA telemetry: 2-4 second scan rate
  - AMI metering: 15-minute intervals
  - PMU measurements: 10-60 frames per second (IEEE C37.118)

Section 6: Grid Monitoring Best Practices

  1. Deploy voltage monitoring at every DER POI above 100 kW.
  2. Install line sensors at 25% intervals along feeders with > 30% DER.
  3. Configure SCADA alarms for reverse power flow detection.
  4. Implement state estimation with DER model integration.
  5. Maintain monitoring data retention for minimum 1 year.
  6. Review protection coordination annually for feeders with new DER.
