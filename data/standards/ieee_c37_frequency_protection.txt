IEEE C37.118 — Frequency Protection and Under/Over-Frequency Relay Procedures
===============================================================================

Section 1: Scope

This document specifies frequency protection requirements for synchronous
generators and interconnected power systems per IEEE C37.102 (AC Generator
Protection) and IEEE C37.118.1 (Synchrophasor Measurements). Frequency
deviations beyond normal operating bands indicate generation-load imbalance
and may lead to system instability if not addressed promptly.

Section 2: Normal Operating Frequency Bands

For 50 Hz systems (IEC standard):
  Normal range: 49.80 - 50.20 Hz (±0.4%)
  Caution range: 49.50 - 49.80 Hz or 50.20 - 50.50 Hz
  Alert range: 49.00 - 49.50 Hz or 50.50 - 51.00 Hz
  Emergency range: Below 49.00 Hz or above 51.00 Hz

For 60 Hz systems (IEEE/NERC standard):
  Normal range: 59.95 - 60.05 Hz
  Caution range: 59.90 - 59.95 Hz or 60.05 - 60.10 Hz
  Alert range: 59.50 - 59.90 Hz or 60.10 - 60.50 Hz
  Emergency range: Below 59.50 Hz or above 60.50 Hz

Section 3: Causes of Frequency Deviation

3.1 Under-Frequency Causes
  - Sudden loss of generation (generator trip, fuel supply interruption)
  - Unexpected load increase beyond spinning reserve capacity
  - Islanding of a load-heavy subsystem
  - Transmission line trip disconnecting generation source

3.2 Over-Frequency Causes
  - Sudden load rejection (large load trip or feeder disconnect)
  - Governor control malfunction causing excess generation
  - Islanding of a generation-heavy subsystem

Section 4: Under-Frequency Load Shedding (UFLS)

4.1 UFLS Scheme Design
Per IEEE C37.117 (Guide for Application of UFLS), the UFLS scheme shall:
  - Shed load in discrete stages (3 to 6 stages recommended)
  - Total load shed capacity: 25-35% of system peak load
  - Each stage sheds 5-7% of load
  - Frequency thresholds set with 0.2-0.5 Hz intervals between stages

4.2 UFLS Settings (50 Hz System Example)
  Stage 1: 49.30 Hz — shed 6% of load — time delay: 0.3 s
  Stage 2: 49.00 Hz — shed 7% of load — time delay: 0.3 s
  Stage 3: 48.70 Hz — shed 7% of load — time delay: 0.3 s
  Stage 4: 48.50 Hz — shed 5% of load — time delay: 0.2 s
  Stage 5: 48.20 Hz — shed 5% of load — time delay: 0.2 s (emergency)

4.3 Rate-of-Change-of-Frequency (ROCOF) Protection
ROCOF relays provide faster detection of islanding and severe disturbances.
Setting: df/dt > 0.5 Hz/s for 100 ms — initiates immediate load shed or
generator disconnect. Typical ROCOF relay response time: 80-120 ms.

Section 5: Over-Frequency Protection

5.1 Generator Over-Frequency Limits
Per IEEE C37.102, synchronous generators must operate continuously within
±0.5 Hz of nominal. Sustained operation above 51.0 Hz (50 Hz system) risks:
  - Turbine blade damage from mechanical resonance
  - Turbine overspeed trip activation
  - Transformer core saturation (V/Hz ratio exceeds rated value)

5.2 Over-Frequency Protection Settings
  Stage 1: 50.50 Hz — alarm only — time delay: 5.0 s
  Stage 2: 51.00 Hz — generator runback command — time delay: 1.0 s
  Stage 3: 51.50 Hz — generator trip — time delay: 0.3 s

Section 6: Frequency Measurement Requirements

Per IEEE C37.118.1, synchrophasor-based frequency measurement shall provide:
  - Reporting rate: ≥ 10 frames per second (fps)
  - Frequency accuracy: ≤ 0.005 Hz under steady-state conditions
  - ROCOF accuracy: ≤ 0.01 Hz/s under steady-state conditions
  - Total Vector Error (TVE): ≤ 1% for P-class, ≤ 1% for M-class

Section 7: Operator Response Procedures

  Step 1: Frequency deviation alarm received — confirm on SCADA display.
  Step 2: Determine if deviation is system-wide or localized (island).
  Step 3: For under-frequency — verify UFLS operation, confirm load shed.
  Step 4: For over-frequency — verify governor response, check for load loss.
  Step 5: Coordinate with neighboring control areas (ACE exchange per NERC).
  Step 6: Log event with timestamp, frequency nadir/peak, cause, actions taken.
